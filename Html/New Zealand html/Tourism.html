Home of middle earth

If you are interested in J.R.R Tolkien's works, Lord of the rings and the hobbit, or the films inspired by them, New Zealand is the place for you. Sometimes called "The home of Middle earth", many iconic scenes from The Lord of the Rings trilogy and the Hobbit film were filmed in New Zealand. If you're interested, there are also guided tours of the exact set of Hobbiton available for booking

The Waitomo caves

The Waitomo caves are a series of caves, connected by a river. The caves are known for their large population of glowworms. The glowworms light up the dark caves with blue and green luminescence, and it’s a sight quite unique to this location. As a visitor, you ride down the river in a boat and admire the beauty of these magical creatures. Alternatively, if you’re a thrill seeker, there is an option to go rafting down a different, yet equally beautiful cave.

The Museum of New Zealand

The Museum of New Zealand is, unsurprisingly, the national museum of New Zealand, and is mostly known by the name: Te papa (Māori for “The treasure box”). It’s located in Wellington, New Zealand’s capital. On display in the museum is a vast collection of traditional Māori artwork, along with other pieces of history, such as dinosaur teeth dated back to about 130 million years in the ago
