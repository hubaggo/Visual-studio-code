The Māori people

The Māori people are the indigenous people of New Zealand. The Māori we know today are descendants of settlers who came to New Zealand from east Polynesia between 1320 and 1350. Their relationship with the European settlers varied at the start, but as was sadly often the case in British colonies, this fragile peace didn’t last.
